Product Management

Truthfully, coming from an engineering and academic background, I didn’t know the difference between a product and a project manager before taking this class. All I knew was that both roles were critical in building things that people need, whether they know it or not… and I love building things.

The nice thing about this course is that it was a project driven class, allowing us to “get our hands dirty” and apply what we were learning to a real project. In just 10 weeks we formed teams, conceived an idea centered around a pain point, conducted research with different stakeholders and on the market as a whole, developed a product prototype, and presented our work to our professors and the class.

The pain point we tried to address was something I personally experienced as I moved to Chicago to begin graduate school - it is difficult to find people to play pickup sports with, especially when you are new to a city, don’t know anyone, and really just find it difficult to put yourself out there and walk up to people and say, “can I play?”. For as long as I can remember, I have used pickup sports as a way to stay fit, meet people outside of work or school, and ultimately to build a sense of community wherever I may be living. With MatchUp, our pickup sports app, people can meet new people, stay fit, and find a sense of community in a new city even in the midst of a hectic lifestyle.

We summarized our stakeholder and market research in a Market Requirements Document (click here to see our MRD), which informed the design of our Product Requirements Document (click here to see our PRD). We then communicated all of our work in our Product Proposal Presentation (click here to see our Slide Deck).

I hope you enjoy them! Please reach out if you are in Chicago and want to play any soccer, basketball, frisbee, tennis, or any other pickup sport you can think of!
