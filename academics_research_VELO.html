Velo

Throughout my time in undergrad, and even before during high school, I have always felt a draw towards being a teacher in some way, shape or form. Over the years, I have found so many different ways to do this. I have coached basketball and soccer teams, tutored kids 1 on 1 in math and science, and even taught English to high school students for a year in Thailand after finishing my undergraduate degree.

Fast-forward to today and I still have a heart for teaching in any capacity possible. One way that I have tried to continue to do this is through the creation of low-cost technological tools that can be used in educational settings to teach concepts of all kinds.

During the Design of Technological Tools for Thinking and Learning course, I worked to create a robotics toolkit called VELO. When paired with a custom simulation platform, it can help teach young students about robotics, neuroscience, and biology.

You can find our research paper about the entire robotics platform and simulation here!
