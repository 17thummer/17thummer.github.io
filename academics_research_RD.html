Modular Robots

Coming into graduate school, I wasn’t exactly sure what topic I would be focusing on for my research, though I knew I wanted to be in a place that I could be creative, on the cutting edge of technology, and be challenged to learn new things each and every day.

Then I found robotics.

With an undergraduate degree in mechanical engineering, I had little to no experience working with robots. I didn’t (and still don’t) see that as a negative though. Every single day I am able to learn something new, whether it’s through reading research papers, talking to any of my ridiculously bright peers at Northwestern’s Center for Robotics and Biosystems, or through a seemingly infinite number of design iterations (a.k.a. failures!).

Over the past year and a half, I have gone from having virtually no experience in robotics, to conceiving my own research direction in the field of modular robotics, to writing and publishing my first academic paper. I will be presenting my paper, A non-cubic space-filling modular robot, at this year’s International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) in Yokohama, Japan!

I am super excited to share my paper on a new form of modular robot! Thanks so much for reading!
