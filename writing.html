Lives of Inanimate Objects

In a world dominated by materialism, I often find myself viewing the natural world and the man-made objects around me through an exploitative lens. However unconsicious these thoughts may be, they can have immense influence on the way in which I live my life. The implications of this mindset show up in my behavior - wastefulness, consumerism, and general disregard for the natural world. In times of reflection I begin to question my own behavioral patterns, a pattern that is more common in modern society than any one person would care to admit. This led me to ask the following questions. What if the inanimate objects that humans create had the ability to perceive the world around them, to feel emotions as humans do, and to dream about their future? How can this shift in perspective to the inanimate, which in reality have no control over their own existence, help me understand the implications of my own actions, of which I have complete control over? These short stories are an exploration into these questions in reflective, sometimes somber, but generally hopeful ways. I hope you enjoy them.
